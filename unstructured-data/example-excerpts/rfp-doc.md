# 1. Introduction and Background

The **California Public Employees’ Retirement System (CalPERS)** is the largest public pension fund in the United States. As of fiscal year 2025, CalPERS manages approximately **$556.2 billion in total assets** on behalf of **2.3 million members**, including public employees, retirees, and their beneficiaries. The System currently maintains a **funded status of approximately 79%**, and its long-term investment strategy is designed to ensure the sustainability of retirement benefits while managing risk across market cycles.

CalPERS maintains a diversified portfolio across public equity, fixed income, private markets, and real assets. Fixed income plays a critical role in the portfolio by providing **income generation, capital preservation, and liability stabilization**.

In 2025, the CalPERS Investment Office initiated a **comprehensive review of the Global and Core Fixed Income program**. This review included evaluation of portfolio structure, duration positioning, manager performance, ESG integration practices, and fee competitiveness across the manager roster.

As a result of this review, CalPERS is conducting a search for a **Core Fixed Income manager** to manage approximately **$8 billion** in assets. The selected manager will be expected to deliver consistent benchmark-relative returns, strong risk management, and best-in-class ESG integration consistent with CalPERS investment beliefs.

The **benchmark for the mandate will be the Bloomberg U.S. Aggregate Bond Index**.

Wilshire Advisors and Meketa Investment Group are assisting CalPERS in conducting this search.

# 2. Mandate Requirements

## Investment Objectives

The primary objective of the Core Fixed Income mandate is to generate **consistent risk-adjusted returns in excess of the Bloomberg U.S. Aggregate Bond Index** over a full market cycle.

The selected manager will be expected to:

- Deliver consistent benchmark-relative performance
- Provide diversification relative to equity market risk
- Support CalPERS' long-term liability-matching objectives
- Maintain strong liquidity and capital preservation characteristics

The mandate will be benchmark-aware and should maintain exposure consistent with the broad characteristics of the Bloomberg U.S. Aggregate Bond Index.

---

## Risk Parameters

CalPERS expects the selected manager to operate within clearly defined risk parameters, including:

- **Tracking error:** generally expected to remain within **1.0%–2.0% annualized**
- **Duration:** maintained within a reasonable range relative to the benchmark
- **Credit quality:** portfolio must maintain investment-grade characteristics
- **Sector exposures:** should not materially deviate from benchmark exposures without strong investment rationale

Managers must demonstrate a robust and disciplined **risk management framework**, including scenario analysis, stress testing, and interest rate risk management.

---

## ESG Integration Requirements

Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) integration is a **mandatory requirement** for this mandate.

Respondents must demonstrate that ESG considerations are **systematically embedded into the investment process**, including but not limited to:

- Integration of **ESG factors into credit analysis**
- Evaluation of **issuer governance practices**
- Assessment of **climate-related financial risks**
- Sovereign ESG analysis, including governance and political risk indicators
- Clear processes for identifying ESG-related investment risks and opportunities

Managers must also provide:

- Detailed documentation of ESG integration methodologies
- Examples of investment decisions influenced by ESG considerations
- Evidence that ESG integration is implemented consistently across the portfolio

Proposals lacking clear and robust ESG integration will **not be considered**.

---

## Reporting Requirements

Selected managers will be required to provide comprehensive reporting to CalPERS on a regular basis, including:

- Monthly performance reporting
- Detailed attribution analysis
- Risk analytics and portfolio exposures
- ESG integration reporting
- Portfolio holdings transparency
- Compliance reporting

Managers must demonstrate the ability to provide **institutional-grade reporting suitable for public pension governance oversight**.

---

## Fee Structure Expectations

CalPERS expects highly competitive institutional pricing for this mandate.

For a mandate of this size, CalPERS generally expects **management fees below 12 basis points (bps)** for Core Fixed Income strategies.

Proposals should clearly outline:

- Base management fee
- Breakpoint schedules
- Any performance-based components (if applicable)
- All additional costs and expenses

Fee competitiveness will be a key component of the evaluation process.

# 3. Evaluation Criteria

Proposals will be evaluated according to the following criteria and weights:

| Evaluation Criteria | Weight |
|---|---|
| Investment Performance | 30% |
| ESG Integration | 20% |
| Team Stability and Experience | 15% |
| Risk Management Framework | 15% |
| Fee Competitiveness | 10% |
| Reporting and Transparency | 10% |

CalPERS reserves the right to request additional information during the evaluation process.

# 4. Required Information from Respondents

Respondents must provide the following information:

### Firm Overview
- Firm history and ownership structure
- Total assets under management
- Assets managed in Core Fixed Income strategies

### Investment Strategy
- Detailed description of the investment process
- Portfolio construction methodology
- Duration and yield curve positioning framework

### Performance Track Record
- Minimum **five-year performance history**
- Benchmark-relative returns
- Risk metrics and attribution analysis

### ESG Integration
- Formal ESG policy
- ESG integration methodology
- Case studies demonstrating ESG-informed investment decisions

### Key Personnel
- Portfolio managers and analysts
- Team structure and experience
- Staff turnover statistics

### Fee Proposal
- Detailed fee schedule
- Breakpoints and institutional pricing structure

### Client References
- At least three institutional client references
- Preference for public pension or sovereign clients
