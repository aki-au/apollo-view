## Agenda Item: Global Fixed Income Program Review

**Stephen Gilmore (CIO)** opened the discussion with a review of the Global Fixed Income portfolio and its role within the broader CalPERS asset allocation framework. He noted that recent volatility in global equity markets had underscored the stabilizing function of fixed income within the total portfolio.

Mr. Gilmore stated that staff had been evaluating opportunities to modestly increase exposure to longer-duration securities as part of an effort to improve liability matching characteristics.

> “Given the plan’s long-term obligations,” Mr. Gilmore noted, “extending duration modestly can help align the asset portfolio with the liability profile while also serving as a stabilizing force during periods of equity market turbulence.”

**President Theresa Taylor** asked staff to elaborate on how duration extension might affect the plan’s overall risk posture.

Mr. Gilmore responded that staff analysis suggested the move would improve portfolio resilience during downturns, particularly if equity volatility continued.

**Frank Miller (Vice President)** expressed general support for the direction.

> “We’ve talked for some time about the need to strengthen the liability-matching component of the portfolio,” Mr. Miller stated. “This seems like a logical evolution, particularly if equity markets remain unsettled.”

**David Miller** asked whether current external managers were positioned appropriately to execute the duration extension strategy.

Mr. Gilmore confirmed that most existing managers had the flexibility to adjust portfolio duration within their mandates, though staff would continue monitoring positioning closely.

---

### ESG Integration Discussion

**Stacie Olivares** raised a question regarding environmental, social, and governance considerations within fixed income mandates.

> “As we look at expanding allocations here,” Ms. Olivares said, “I want to ensure that ESG considerations are being integrated into sovereign and corporate bond selection. Fixed income markets carry significant exposure to climate and governance risks.”

**Tom Toth (Wilshire Advisors)** responded that ESG integration in fixed income had improved materially across the industry over the past several years.

He noted that many managers now incorporate sovereign governance metrics, climate exposure, and corporate sustainability indicators into credit analysis.

> “In our view,” Mr. Toth said, “CalPERS managers are generally aligned with best practices, though there may be opportunities to strengthen reporting around ESG-related risk factors.”

**Sharris Jones** asked whether staff expected ESG considerations to materially influence manager selection in future mandates.

Mr. Gilmore responded that ESG integration was one of several factors considered alongside investment performance, risk management, and operational capabilities.

---

### Fee Competitiveness

**Eraina Ortega** asked staff whether the current fixed income manager roster remained fee competitive relative to industry peers.

> “Given the scale of this fund,” Ms. Ortega said, “we should be able to secure highly competitive pricing. I’d like reassurance that we’re benchmarking fees appropriately.”

Mr. Gilmore confirmed that staff regularly reviewed fee arrangements and worked with consultants to benchmark pricing across comparable mandates.

**Steve McCourt (Meketa Investment Group)** added that CalPERS generally benefits from strong negotiating leverage due to the size of its mandates.

> “Our assessment is that the program remains competitive from a fee standpoint,” Mr. McCourt said, “though there are always opportunities to revisit pricing as mandates mature.”

---

## Committee Discussion

Several board members expressed general support for the role of fixed income as a stabilizing element within the portfolio.

**President Taylor** summarized the discussion by noting that the committee appeared broadly supportive of staff continuing to evaluate duration extension opportunities.

> “This committee understands the importance of balancing growth assets with assets that help stabilize the plan during volatility,” she said.
