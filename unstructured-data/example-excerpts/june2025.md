## Agenda Item: Global Fixed Income Program Update

**Stephen Gilmore (CIO)** provided an update on the Global Fixed Income program and recent portfolio performance. He noted that while fixed income had provided some diversification benefits earlier in the year, the evolving interest rate environment had introduced additional considerations for the program.

Mr. Gilmore reported that staff continued to view fixed income as an important stabilizing component of the portfolio but acknowledged that duration positioning required careful monitoring.

> “While duration extension remains relevant from a liability perspective,” Mr. Gilmore said, “we must also remain mindful of potential rate volatility and its impact on portfolio performance.”

---

### Duration Risk Discussion

**David Miller** asked staff whether the portfolio’s duration exposure could create downside risk if interest rates moved higher than expected.

> “Earlier this year we talked about extending duration,” Mr. Miller noted. “Given how quickly the macro environment is shifting, how confident are we that this remains the right positioning?”

Mr. Gilmore responded that staff had not materially increased duration yet and were continuing to evaluate the appropriate balance between liability matching and interest rate risk.

**Frank Miller (Vice President)** stated that he remained generally supportive of fixed income’s role in the portfolio but acknowledged that conditions had become more complex.

> “It’s still an important stabilizer,” he said, “but we need to ensure we’re not locking ourselves into the wrong rate environment.”

---

### ESG Integration

**Stacie Olivares** returned to the topic of ESG integration within fixed income mandates.

> “At the last meeting we discussed ESG considerations at a high level,” Ms. Olivares said. “I’d like to see stronger evidence of how those considerations are actually influencing portfolio construction.”

**Tom Toth (Wilshire Advisors)** noted that several managers had recently expanded their ESG frameworks, particularly in sovereign bond analysis.

However, he acknowledged that implementation across managers was not uniform.

**Sharris Jones** stated that the committee should expect consistent ESG integration across mandates.

> “If ESG is important to this board,” she said, “we should be able to clearly see it reflected in manager processes.”

---

### Manager Performance Review

Mr. Gilmore then turned to performance monitoring within the Global Fixed Income roster.

He noted that one manager—referred to as **Manager C** in the presentation materials—had underperformed the benchmark over multiple evaluation periods.

> “Staff is monitoring the situation closely,” Mr. Gilmore said. “While performance has lagged recently, we believe it is appropriate to continue evaluating the manager’s positioning before making any structural decisions.”

**Eraina Ortega** asked whether the underperformance was attributable to duration positioning or security selection.

Mr. Gilmore stated that the primary driver appeared to be macro positioning related to global interest rate expectations.

---

### Consultant Perspective

**Steve McCourt (Meketa Investment Group)** advised the committee that the evolving market environment presented an appropriate opportunity to review the overall structure of the fixed income program.

> “Given recent volatility,” Mr. McCourt said, “it may be prudent for the board to consider whether the current manager structure remains optimal.”

He noted that several large public pension plans had recently undertaken similar reviews.

---

## Committee Discussion

Board members expressed mixed views regarding the outlook for fixed income.

While several members continued to support the asset class as a stabilizing force, others emphasized the importance of monitoring duration exposure carefully.

---

## Action Items

1. **Investment Staff** to conduct further analysis on interest rate sensitivity within the Global Fixed Income portfolio.  
2. **Consultants** to provide a comparative review of fixed income program structures used by peer pension funds.  
3. **Investment Staff** to continue monitoring Manager C performance and report back to the committee.  
4. **Staff** to provide enhanced ESG integration reporting across fixed income mandates.
