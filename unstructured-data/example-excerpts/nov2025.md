## Agenda Item: Global Fixed Income Program Review and Manager Oversight

**Stephen Gilmore (CIO)** opened the discussion by reviewing recent developments in the Global Fixed Income portfolio. He noted that continued volatility in global interest rates had placed increased scrutiny on duration exposure across the program.

Mr. Gilmore stated that while earlier discussions in the year had considered extending duration to better align with liabilities, staff now believed a more cautious posture was appropriate.

> “The macro environment has evolved considerably since March,” Mr. Gilmore said. “Given persistent inflation uncertainty and central bank divergence, staff believes it is prudent to carefully reassess the program’s duration profile.”

---

### Duration Risk Concerns

Several board members expressed concern regarding the potential risks associated with longer-duration exposures.

**David Miller** stated that the committee needed to revisit earlier assumptions.

> “Earlier this year we were discussing extending duration,” he said. “Looking at the rate volatility we’ve experienced since then, I’m not sure that view holds today.”

**Frank Miller (Vice President)** agreed, noting that the committee must remain adaptable as market conditions evolve.

**Eraina Ortega** asked staff to quantify the potential downside risk associated with the current duration profile.

Mr. Gilmore responded that staff was conducting additional scenario analysis to evaluate how the portfolio might perform under various rate paths.

---

### Manager C Performance Review

Mr. Gilmore then addressed the performance of **Manager C**, which had been discussed in prior meetings.

He reported that following the most recent performance evaluation, staff had recommended placing the manager on **probationary status**.

> “Manager C has continued to lag the benchmark over multiple evaluation periods,” Mr. Gilmore said. “Staff believes probation is an appropriate step while we assess whether the strategy remains aligned with the program.”

---

### ESG Integration Discussion

**Stacie Olivares** raised concerns about ESG integration within fixed income mandates.

> “We’ve discussed ESG in this program several times,” Ms. Olivares said. “What I’m still not seeing is clear evidence that these factors are systematically influencing investment decisions.”

She asked staff and consultants to provide more detailed reporting regarding ESG-related exposures within the fixed income portfolio.

**Tom Toth (Wilshire Advisors)** acknowledged that ESG implementation varied across managers and stated that improving reporting transparency would likely benefit the board’s oversight process.

---

### Potential RFP

Mr. Gilmore stated that staff was evaluating whether the current environment warranted opening a search for an additional core fixed income manager.

> “Given the probationary status of Manager C and the evolving macro environment,” he said, “staff believes it may be appropriate to consider initiating a request for proposals.”

---

### Fee Review

**Steve McCourt (Meketa Investment Group)** recommended that CalPERS take the opportunity to review fee structures across the entire fixed income roster.

> “Given the scale of this program,” Mr. McCourt said, “there may be opportunities to renegotiate fee arrangements as mandates mature.”

---

## Committee Discussion

Committee members broadly agreed that the fixed income program warranted closer oversight given current market conditions.

Several members emphasized the importance of balancing liability considerations with interest rate risk.

---

## Action Items

1. **Investment Staff** to conduct scenario analysis on duration exposure and interest rate risk.  
2. **Staff and Consultants** to prepare detailed ESG integration reporting for fixed income mandates.  
3. **Investment Staff** to initiate preparations for a potential core fixed income RFP.  
4. **Consultants** to conduct a comprehensive fee benchmarking review across the fixed income manager roster.  
5. **Investment Staff** to provide ongoing updates regarding Manager C probation status.
